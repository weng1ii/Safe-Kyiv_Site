Objects of culture during the war

On March 7, 2022, the St. George's Church of the Boryspil Diocese of the Ukrainian Orthodox Church, which was built in 1873, burned down as a result of shelling in the village of Zavorichi, Brovar District, Kyiv Region.

On the night of February 27-28, as a result of hostilities in Kyiv, the church in honor of St. Joasaph of Belgorod on International Square was damaged.

On March 5, in the village of Yasnohorodka (Kyiv region), the Church of the Nativity of the Blessed Virgin Mary was damaged during shelling. Two shells hit the dome, the bell tower was damaged by the BMP shell. The parish house built near the church was also damaged. The windows and entrance doors of the temple were stitched from a large-caliber machine gun. (Photos of the damage are currently unavailable.)

On March 5, as a result of shelling, the Ascension Church in the village of Bobryk, Kyiv region, was damaged.

University of the State Fiscal Service of Ukraine until February 24, 2022

The State Fiscal Service University of Ukraine is one of the largest educational institutions in terms of territory and developed infrastructure, which is equipped according to European standards in the park zone of Irpin. UDFSU forms a powerful qualification base of fiscal service employees throughout Ukraine. During its existence, the educational institution trained more than 70,000 specialists.

University of the State Fiscal Service of Ukraine after February 24, 2022

The destroyed University of the State Fiscal Service of Ukraine in Irpen (Kyiv region), May 28, 2022. On the night of February 24, 2022, Russia launched a full-scale war with Ukraine, Putin called it a "military operation." Shelling and bombing of Ukrainian cities began. Russian occupiers and war criminals turned Irpin, a cozy suburb of Kyiv, into a ghost town. On March 28, Irpin was liberated from the Russian invaders.

Help the cultural objects of Ukraine!

After the invasion, the Russian Federation damaged or destroyed almost half a thousand objects of cultural heritage. According to the Ministry of Culture, in particular, 139 objects of cultural heritage of Ukraine - monuments of national and local importance, as well as objects of valuable historical buildings - were destroyed and damaged.
